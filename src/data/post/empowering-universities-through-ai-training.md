<p>I’ve&nbsp;found myself this week trying to think of a catchy, yet&nbsp;accurate&nbsp;tagline for our new company –&nbsp;<a href="/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">WorkSmart-AI</a>.&nbsp;After considering many options (admittedly with the&nbsp;assistance&nbsp;of AI!)&nbsp;and whittling that down to a shortlist, I found myself making a case for a tagline that&nbsp;contained&nbsp;the word “empower”.&nbsp;</p>

<p>I’m&nbsp;not normally one for big, dramatic wording, but this word resonated with so many of my&nbsp;thoughts&nbsp;recently&nbsp;about&nbsp;the importance of&nbsp;having open and honest debates&nbsp;about how&nbsp;society collectively&nbsp;shapes&nbsp;the future&nbsp;of&nbsp;AI.&nbsp;Of critical&nbsp;significance to my area of work, how we need to&nbsp;recognise&nbsp;the&nbsp;importance&nbsp;of&nbsp;involving&nbsp;all stakeholders in the conversation about its impact&nbsp;on&nbsp;the workplace and&nbsp;the HE&nbsp;sector&nbsp;moving forwards.&nbsp;</p>

<p>But open and honest discussions&nbsp;around AI&nbsp;can&nbsp;surely&nbsp;only be had between people who are informed with the facts and knowledge&nbsp;needed to join in. Unless they are&nbsp;“empowered” with the relevant information and skills, then how is it possible for people to be confident in their&nbsp;thoughts&nbsp;and decisions?&nbsp;</p>

<p>Additionally, having spent several decades working with&nbsp;colleagues&nbsp;in education, an overriding reality is that no one likes being “done to”.&nbsp;It’s&nbsp;a sure-fire way to get&nbsp;people’s&nbsp;backs up and&nbsp;instantly create opposition to any of your ideas.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Much better to involve people in the decision-making process,&nbsp;(whether&nbsp;that’s&nbsp;which AI software to use or strategic approach to take for example)&nbsp;informing them of options and communicating clearly at each step of the journey towards&nbsp;making a decision.&nbsp;Ultimately it&nbsp;may not be the&nbsp;decision that&nbsp;they’d&nbsp;have chosen, but&nbsp;(hopefully)&nbsp;they’ll&nbsp;be a lot more supportive&nbsp;through understanding the rationale&nbsp;and feeling listened to.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Bringing it back to the word “empower”,&nbsp;I feel this&nbsp;encapsulates nicely how I feel towards the value&nbsp;of&nbsp;the AI training that we&nbsp;at&nbsp;<a href="/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">WorkSmart-AI</a>&nbsp;provide to universities. It arms people with the&nbsp;information and skills&nbsp;(and thereby power)&nbsp;to be able to understand the&nbsp;opportunities and challenges that&nbsp;AI&nbsp;poses to their roles and the wider&nbsp;HE&nbsp;sector.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<p>It also critically&nbsp;empowers&nbsp;those in leadership&nbsp;with the&nbsp;confidence&nbsp;to&nbsp;embrace&nbsp;others&#8217;&nbsp;opinions and ideas,&nbsp;ensuring a more&nbsp;productive and robust decision-making process.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<p>The tagline that&nbsp;we’ve&nbsp;therefore decided upon? &#8211; ‘Empowering universities through AI training’.&nbsp;</p>
