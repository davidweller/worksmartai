<p>As a former journalist I feel very strongly about this subject. I have always enjoyed writing. Being able to write down exactly my thoughts or feelings on a subject is liberating. As anyone who knows my style of writing, I like to write as if&nbsp;I’m&nbsp;talking to someone – a conversation on a page!&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<p>However, on the introduction of AI into my workplace and increasingly my home, a question has loomed in my head around how much writing should I&nbsp;actually be&nbsp;doing from scratch? When I consider how quickly and easily AI can&nbsp;write&nbsp;an article, compared to the length of time and effort it would take me – where does the value lie?&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<p>And&nbsp;I think this&nbsp;is the nub of a wider conversation that needs to be had, not just within our own heads, but also within homes,&nbsp;workplaces&nbsp;and board rooms. “What do we&nbsp;<strong>want</strong>&nbsp;AI to be doing and what do we&nbsp;<strong>not want</strong>&nbsp;it to be doing?”&nbsp;</p>

<p>I often speak to people on their use of AI and what is&nbsp;very clear, is that we all&nbsp;seem to use&nbsp;it differently! When focusing specifically on the task of writing, it turns out that many people&nbsp;don’t&nbsp;enjoy writing at all. They find it a laborious and&nbsp;difficult task.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<p>Staff with dyslexia for example have told me that they struggle to get started when&nbsp;writing&nbsp;an article. They therefore find AI is incredibly helpful at suggesting to them&nbsp;an initial&nbsp;starting point, which they then expand upon. This not only saves them a lot of time, but it also reduces their anxiety with completing a writing task and helps them feel more confident overall.&nbsp;That’s&nbsp;a massive win!&nbsp;</p>

<p>Others&nbsp;I’ve&nbsp;spoken to like to use AI to enhance the work that&nbsp;they’ve&nbsp;already written.&nbsp;That’s&nbsp;another massive win!&nbsp;</p>

<p>For me however, when it comes to my words that&nbsp;I’ve&nbsp;written, I&nbsp;don’t&nbsp;want AI coming anywhere near it.&nbsp;At the moment&nbsp;(and never say never), even after various prompting trials, I&nbsp;haven’t&nbsp;found AI to be able to mimic my exact style. I also recognise the value of the enjoyment that I get from writing&#8230;..so for me,&nbsp;maintaining&nbsp;my voice and love of writing is currently my win.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>

<p>&#8230;.All&nbsp;of that said, do I use AI in&nbsp;practically every&nbsp;other&nbsp;facet&nbsp;of my working (and mostly personal) life &#8211; absolutely!!! And will the value of my writing change as AI improves and other pressures on my time increase &#8211; who knows?&nbsp;</p>
